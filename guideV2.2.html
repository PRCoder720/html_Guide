Beginner's Guide to Our Products & Features

Welcome to Autobongs! If you're new to smoke shop products the variety can feel overwhelming. We're here to make it easy and straightforward.


Consumption materials come in two main forms: dried herbs (dried plant material) and concentrated herbs (purified extracts like oils or waxes, concentrated versions of the dried form). Each works with different devices designed specifically for that material type.

Bongs (Water Pipes)

Water pipes use water filtration to cool and filter smoke before you inhale, creating a smoother experience that's easier on your throat and lungs. If you’re sensitive to harsh smoke, a bong is often the most comfortable place to start with traditional combustion.

Common styles: beaker bongs for stability and larger water volume, straight-tube bongs that clear quickly and are easy to clean, recycler bongs with looping water paths for extra cooling and flavor, mini/travel bongs for compact setups, and percolator bongs with built-in “percs” (honeycomb, tree, showerhead, etc.) for added diffusion.

Materials: borosilicate glass for clean flavor and visual appeal, silicone for flexible, nearly unbreakable travel pieces, and glass/silicone hybrid designs that mix durability with a premium feel.

Best for:

  • People who want smoother, cooler hits than a dry pipe or joint.
  • At-home or session-style use with friends.
  • Users who enjoy the ritual of packing, lighting, and clearing a water pipe.

Not as good for:

  • Maximum portability and pockets-only setups.
  • Very discreet use, since bongs produce larger clouds and some smell.
  • Anyone who never wants to deal with water changes or glass cleaning.

Typical price ranges: Budget pieces around $30–$70, mid-range bongs with thicker glass and percs around $70–$200, and premium or artist-made “heady” pieces $200 and up.

Why try a bong: If you want classic smoke with maximum smoothness, bongs deliver. They’re highly customizable, fun to collect, and—when cared for—can be a long-lasting centerpiece of your setup.

Bubblers (Compact Water Pipes)

Bubblers provide the same water-filtered smoothness as traditional bongs but in a portable, handheld size. These compact water pipes are easy to use anywhere and offer excellent portability without sacrificing filtration quality.

Common styles: hammer bubblers that sit flat and feel solid in the hand, Sherlock-style bubblers with a curved neck for a classic look, side-car bubblers that reduce water splashback, and mini bubblers that stay small while still using a bit of water to cool smoke.

Materials: glass for clean flavor and a clear view of smoke and water, silicone for tougher, travel-ready pieces, and glass/silicone combos that balance durability and taste.

Best for:

  • Users who want some water filtration but not a full-size bong.
  • On-the-go or small-space use where you can still access a little water.
  • People who like easy one-hand operation and straightforward function.

Not as good for:

  • Huge, extra-long pulls (they have smaller chambers than big bongs).
  • Ultra-discreet pocket carry (still bulkier than a tiny pipe or vape pen).
  • Anyone who wants to avoid water completely.

Typical price ranges: Budget bubblers around $25–$60, mid-range options with percs or thicker glass $60–$120, and premium or branded styles $120 and up.

Why try a bubbler: Bubblers sit right between a pipe and a bong. If you want smoother hits than a dry pipe while keeping things compact and simple, a bubbler is the perfect hybrid choice.

Hand Pipes

Hand pipes are the simplest smoking option available. These small, portable devices require no water or complicated setup. Perfect for beginners and experienced users alike.

Common styles: spoon pipes with the classic bowl-and-stem shape, chillums and one-hitters for quick, tiny hits, Sherlock pipes with a curved neck, steamrollers for strong, fast-clearing hits, and novelty or character pipes that bring fun shapes and themes.

Materials: glass for clean flavor and endless designs, metal for ultra-durable pocket pieces, wood for a warmer, natural feel, stone or ceramic for artistic and sturdy home pieces, and silicone for almost unbreakable travel pipes.

Best for:

  • Quick, simple sessions with minimal setup.
  • Pocket-friendly, toss-in-a-bag use.
  • Micro-dosing or taking just a hit or two at a time.

Not as good for:

  • People with very sensitive lungs who need lots of cooling.
  • Large group sessions where smoother, bigger hits are preferred.
  • Anyone who dislikes warmer, more direct smoke.

Typical price ranges: Budget pipes around $10–$30, mid-range pieces with thicker glass or nicer materials $30–$80, and premium or artist-made pipes $80 and above.

Why try a hand pipe: Hand pipes are the go-to for ease and convenience. If you want something you can grab, load, and use in seconds, a hand pipe is your most reliable everyday companion.

Dry Herb Vaporizers

Flower vaporizers heat dried herbs to release active ingredients as vapor without combustion. This produces less smoke, less odor, and is easier on your respiratory system compared to traditional smoking methods. Available in portable and desktop models.

Heating styles: conduction (herb sits directly on a heated surface, heats fast), convection (hot air passes through the herb for more even heating and flavor), and hybrid systems that combine both for balanced performance.

Form factors: portable vaporizers for everyday carry, desktop or plug-in units for powerful at-home sessions, and devices designed for either longer “session” use or quick, on-demand hits.

Materials used inside: ceramic ovens for even heating and clean taste, stainless steel ovens for durability and efficient performance, glass airpaths or mouthpieces for smooth flavor, and silicone or plastic exteriors to keep devices light and cool (in quality devices, these are placed away from the hottest parts).

Best for:

  • People who want to reduce smoke and smell while still using flower.
  • Users focused on lung comfort and smoother inhalation.
  • Discreet use with smaller clouds and a more subtle aroma.
  • Those who like dialing in different temperatures for taste and effect.

Not as good for:

  • Folks who love the heavy, dense feel of traditional smoke.
  • Anyone who never wants to maintain or charge a device.
  • Using sticky concentrates—unless the vaporizer is specifically designed for both.

Typical price ranges: Budget vaporizers around $40–$100, mid-range units with better batteries and temp control $100–$250, and premium desktop or flagship portables $250 and above.

Why try a dry herb vaporizer: If you want to treat your lungs more gently while still enjoying flower, vaporizers are an excellent upgrade. They’re efficient, more discreet, and give you more control over how each session feels.

Dab Rigs (Concentrate Water Pipes)

Dab rigs are water pipes specifically designed for concentrated products. Since concentrates are more potent than flower, you typically need only one or two inhalations per session. Dab rigs are available in traditional styles (torch-heated), electric models (battery-powered), or convertible designs that work with both materials.

Common styles: standard glass rigs with a joint for a banger or nail, recycler rigs that loop water for extra cooling and smoothness, mini or micro rigs that keep vapor dense and flavorful, and e-rigs that build heat and control into an all-in-one electronic package.

Materials: borosilicate glass rigs for clean flavor, quartz bangers for excellent heat retention and flavor, titanium or ceramic nails for durability and specific heating characteristics, and silicone rigs with glass or quartz inserts for tough, travel-ready setups.

Best for:

  • People who regularly use concentrates and want proper tools.
  • Strong effects in just one or two hits.
  • Flavor hunters who like exploring different temperatures.

Not as good for:

  • Complete beginners who aren’t ready for concentrate potency.
  • Very discreet use, since rigs still produce visible vapor and some smell.
  • Anyone who doesn’t want to learn torch timing (unless they choose an e-rig).

Typical price ranges: Budget rigs around $50–$120, mid-range rigs and some e-rigs $120–$300, and premium heady glass or flagship e-rigs $300 and up.

Why try a dab rig: If you’re already using concentrates or want to explore them, a dedicated rig provides cleaner flavor, better control, and more efficient use of your material than improvised setups.

Bong Techniques: Cooling Your Hits

As you get more comfortable with water pipes, you can start experimenting with ways to make hits cooler and smoother. Small changes in how you use your bong can noticeably change how it feels.

Ice catchers and ice: some bongs have pinched sections or “ice catchers” that hold ice cubes in the neck. As smoke passes over the ice, it cools down before it reaches your mouth, which can feel gentler on your throat.

Glycerin coils and attachments: certain mouthpieces or coils are filled with glycerin and designed to be chilled in the freezer. Glycerin doesn’t freeze solid like water, so these pieces can stay cold longer and cool the smoke without adding extra meltwater.

Water level and temperature: adjusting how much water you use and whether it’s room temp, cool, or slightly chilled can also change the feel of your hits. Too much water can add drag, while too little may not filter enough.

Best for:

  • Users who already like bongs but want smoother, cooler pulls.
  • People with sensitive throats who value comfort.
  • Experimenters who enjoy fine-tuning their setup.

Not as good for:

  • Very minimal setups where you don’t want extra parts to chill or swap.
  • Anyone who prefers simple, room-temperature hits and easy cleanup.

Why try advanced bong techniques: Cooling your hits with ice catchers, glycerin pieces, or tuned water levels can make your bong feel like a different piece—smoother, softer, and more tailored to what you enjoy.

Percolators: Different Ways to Filter and Diffuse

Percolators, or “percs,” are internal structures in bongs and rigs that break your smoke or vapor into smaller bubbles. More bubbles mean more surface area, which usually means smoother, cooler hits.

Common percolator types:

  • Tree percs: multiple arms that bubble up like branches, offering lots of diffusion.
  • Showerhead percs: a round disc or dome with slits around the edge, creating even, circular bubbling.
  • Honeycomb percs: flat discs full of small holes that make tons of tiny bubbles.
  • Inline percs: horizontal tubes with slits along the length, great for smooth, stable diffusion.
  • Matrix or stereo percs: complex grid-like structures that create very dense diffusion.

Best for:

  • Users who want smoother, more refined hits and extra cooling.
  • People who enjoy watching intricate diffusion and function.
  • Finding the balance between smoothness and the amount of draw resistance you like.

Not as good for:

  • Those who want ultra-simple, easy-to-clean pieces with minimal internal parts.
  • Users who don’t like extra drag or resistance when they pull.

Why learn about percs: Understanding different percolators helps you pick a bong or rig that fits your preference—whether you like airy and simple, or dense and ultra-smooth.

How to Dab Using a Banger on a Dab Rig

Dabbing with a banger on a dab rig is all about timing, gentle heat, and staying safe around hot glass or quartz. Once you learn the basic flow, it becomes a simple, repeatable ritual.

What you’ll need: a dab rig with a banger, a dab tool, carb cap, small amount of concentrate, and isopropyl alcohol or cleaning swabs for after your hit.

Basic steps:

  1. Fill your rig so the water just covers the percolator slits or holes—enough to bubble, but not splash into your mouth.
  2. Use your dab tool to pick up a small amount of concentrate and set the tool aside where it won’t touch anything.
  3. Heat the outside and bottom of the banger evenly until it’s visibly hot (avoid pointing the flame at joints or one spot for too long).
  4. Let the banger cool down for a short moment so it’s not glowing hot—this helps protect flavor and reduces harshness.
  5. Gently place the concentrate into the warm banger while you inhale through the rig, keeping your pull slow and steady.
  6. Place the carb cap over the banger to direct airflow and spin the vapor around, then finish your inhale.
  7. Exhale, then use a cotton swab (dry or with a little isopropyl, once cool enough) to wipe out any leftover residue.

Tips for smoother dabs:

  • Start with very small dabs until you know your comfort level.
  • Keep your banger clean so each session tastes fresh.
  • Always handle hot glass carefully and set your rig on a stable, flat surface.

How to Use a Butane Torch to Heat a Dab Rig

A butane torch makes it easy to get your banger or nail up to temperature, but it’s important to use it calmly and safely. Think of it like using a kitchen torch—controlled, focused, and never rushed.

Before you start:

  • Make sure your rig is on a stable, heat-resistant surface.
  • Check that your torch is filled with butane, pointed away from your face, hair, clothing, and anything flammable.
  • Know where the flame adjustment and ignition buttons are before you light it.

Heating with a torch:

  1. Hold the torch so the flame points at the outside and bottom of the banger or nail, not directly at joints or the rig itself.
  2. Move the flame in small circles or passes so you heat the surface evenly instead of roasting one spot.
  3. Turn off the torch as soon as the surface is fully heated—keep it pointed away from people while you click it off.
  4. Set the torch down in a safe place where it can’t roll, fall, or tip.
  5. Allow the banger a little time to cool before applying concentrate so it isn’t painfully hot to inhale from.

Safety tips:

  • Never leave a lit torch unattended.
  • Keep torches, hot glass, and rigs out of reach of kids and pets.
  • Let all hot surfaces cool fully before you try to move or clean them.

How to Use an Electric Nail (E-Nail)

An electric nail replaces the torch with a controller and heating coil, keeping your dab surface at a steady temperature. Once it’s set up, the process is very consistent and beginner-friendly.

What you’ll need: a dab rig, compatible e-nail (coil and controller or all-in-one unit), power outlet, dab tool, carb cap, and a small amount of concentrate.

Basic steps:

  1. Attach the e-nail coil or head to the compatible banger or nail according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  2. Plug the controller into a safe power outlet and turn it on.
  3. Set your desired temperature on the controller—many people start on the lower side for smoother, more flavorful hits.
  4. Wait for the controller to indicate that it has reached temperature (most units have a display or light).
  5. Load a small amount of concentrate onto your dab tool, then gently place it onto the heated surface while inhaling through the rig.
  6. Use your carb cap to direct airflow and finish your hit, then exhale.
  7. After your session, turn off and unplug the controller and allow the nail and coil to cool completely before touching or moving them.

Good habits with e-nails:

  • Start at lower temps and adjust slowly until you find what feels best.
  • Keep the nail clean by gently wiping out residue between hits once it cools a bit.
  • Never wrap or compress a hot coil—let it cool fully before moving or storing.

How to Take a Hit on a Bong

Taking a comfortable bong hit is about pacing yourself—steady breathing, gentle lighting, and knowing when to clear the chamber. With a little practice, it feels smooth and intuitive.

Before you light:

  • Fill the bong with enough water to cover the percolator slits or downstem holes.
  • Make sure the bowl is packed (but not too tight) and seated firmly in the joint.
  • Sit or stand somewhere stable so you can hold the bong securely.

Taking the hit:

  1. Place your mouth inside the mouthpiece, forming a gentle seal with your lips.
  2. As you start a slow inhale, bring the lighter to the edge of the bowl—try to light just a small corner at a time.
  3. Keep your inhale steady so the chamber fills with thick but manageable smoke, not all at once.
  4. When there’s enough smoke in the chamber, stop lighting and remove the bowl or release the carb (if your piece has one).
  5. Inhale the smoke from the chamber in a smooth, controlled breath to “clear” the bong.
  6. Exhale comfortably and give yourself a moment before deciding whether you want another hit.

Comfort tips:

  • Start with small hits until you know how strong your bong and material are.
  • Use cooler water or ice (if your piece supports it) if your throat is sensitive.
  • Clean your bong regularly so hits stay smooth and taste fresh.

How to Pack a Bowl

Packing a bowl well helps your herb burn evenly, pull smoothly, and taste better. You don’t need to overthink it—just aim for a balance between airflow and support.

What you’ll need: ground dry herb, a pipe or bong bowl, and optionally a small screen if your piece uses one.

Basic steps:

  1. Grind your herb so it’s broken up but not powdered—small, fluffy pieces work best.
  2. If your bowl uses a screen, place it flat at the bottom of the bowl.
  3. Sprinkle a small pinch of herb into the bottom and gently tap it down so it forms a light base.
  4. Add another layer or two, pressing only lightly—enough to hold together but not so tight that air can’t pass through.
  5. Check airflow by taking a test pull without lighting. If it feels clogged, gently loosen the top layer with your fingers or tool.
  6. Once it feels right, you’re ready to light a corner and start your session.

Packing tips:

  • For solo sessions, use smaller bowls or partial packs to keep things fresh.
  • For sharing, a slightly fuller bowl can work as long as it still pulls easily.
  • Avoid grinding too fine, which can clog screens and reduce airflow.

Electric Portable Wax Vapes (Dab Pens)

Electric portable wax vapes (often called dab pens) are small battery-powered devices made for cannabis concentrates like wax, shatter, badder, or rosin. They heat a tiny "atomizer" instead of an open flame, so you get quick, potent hits with less smell and more control.

Dry Herb & Concentrated Herb Storage Accessories

How you store your material matters almost as much as how you use it. Good storage keeps your dry herb fresh, your concentrates stable, and your sessions consistent. The right accessories protect against light, air, heat, and sticky messes—so you're not wasting potency or flavor.

For Dry Herb

The goal is to keep it cool, dark, and slightly humid (but not wet):

Airtight Jars

Especially glass with tight lids—help prevent your herb from drying out or getting stale.

UV-Protective or Opaque Containers

Block light, which can degrade cannabinoids and terpenes over time.

Help keep your herb in a comfortable range so it doesn't become crispy or overly moist.

Smell-Proof Containers or Bags

Great if you need extra discretion and don't want the whole room announcing what you bought.

For Concentrated Herb (Dabs, Waxes, Rosin, etc.)

You're fighting heat and stickiness:

Non-Stick Silicone Containers

Make it easy to scoop, load, and finish your concentrates without losing half of it to the walls.

Glass Jars with Tight Lids

Excellent for preserving flavor and texture, especially for higher-end rosin or live resin.

Cool Storage Spots

Small, insulated areas away from direct sun or hot windows help keep concentrates from getting runny or separating.

💡 Bottom Line: Using dedicated storage accessories means less waste, better flavor, and more predictable sessions. Instead of dried-out flower or overheated concentrate stuck to a random piece of parchment, your material stays organized, potent, and ready to go whenever you are.

Herb Grinders & Milling Grinders: How They Work and Why They Matter

Herb grinders break your dry herb into smaller, even pieces so it burns or vaporizes more smoothly and efficiently. Instead of ripping chunks by hand or using scissors, a grinder gives you a consistent texture that packs better bowls, cones, and chambers.

How to Use a Standard Herb Grinder

Most traditional grinders have two or four pieces with metal or plastic "teeth" inside. Using one is simple:

  1. Open the lid and break your herb into small chunks.
  2. Place the herb around the teeth (not directly in the center where the magnet is).
  3. Close the grinder until it's snug.
  4. Twist the top and bottom in opposite directions for 5–15 seconds, depending on how fine you want it.
  5. Tap gently and open the bottom chamber (if you have one) to access your ground herb.

If your grinder has a kief catcher, some of the fine, powdery trichomes will fall through a screen and collect in the bottom over time.

The Benefits of Using a Grinder

Using a grinder instead of your fingers isn't just about convenience. It can change your whole session:

  • More even burn or vaporization: A consistent grind helps bowls, bongs, pipes, and dry herb vapes heat more evenly.
  • Better airflow: No huge chunks blocking your bowl or vape chamber.
  • Stronger, cleaner hits: Less canoeing in joints and fewer hot spots.
  • Less sticky fingers: You keep more trichomes on the herb and less on your skin.
  • Kief collection: Multi-piece grinders let you save kief for a "bonus" bowl, sprinkle, or special session later.

What Is a Milling Grinder & How Is It Different?

Milling grinders (sometimes called "flower mills" or milling-style grinders) work a little differently from classic tooth grinders.

Tooth Grinder

Uses sharp teeth to shred and tear the herb as you twist.

Milling Grinder

Uses a rotating plate with shaped edges or flutes that gently mills the herb against the walls, like a tiny food mill.

Key Differences You'll Notice

Texture

Milling grinders often produce a fluffier, more uniform grind that's great for dry herb vaporizers and evenly packed bowls.

Less Plant Damage

Instead of aggressively shredding, the milling action can be gentler on trichomes, which may help keep flavor and potency.

Ease of Use

Many milling grinders feel smoother to turn, especially with stickier herb.

🧹
Cleaning

Because there are no sharp teeth and fewer tight corners, milling grinders can be easier to brush out and wipe clean.

Which Should You Choose?

If you like quick, simple grinding and don't mind a slightly rougher texture, a classic tooth-style grinder will do the job. If you're really into vaping dry herb, flavor, and consistency, or you just want smoother twisting and easier maintenance, a milling-style grinder can be a very worthwhile upgrade.

Concentrated Herbs (Oils, Waxes & Dabs)

Master the art of temperature control for the best concentrate experience.

Understanding Temperature Ranges

Concentrated herbs are purified versions of the plant — oils, waxes, shatter, rosin, and other extracts that pack a lot of potency into a tiny amount of material. Because they're so dense and sensitive to heat, temperature control matters even more with concentrates than with dried flower. Getting the temp right affects flavor, smoothness, potency, and how often you have to clean your gear.

For most concentrates, you generally want at least around 375°F for effective vaporization. Below that, you may see a little melt and light vapor, but many people find the effects weak and inconsistent.

Around 450°F is a common "sweet spot" for a lot of users — warm enough to fully vaporize your concentrates, but still cool enough to keep the flavor bright and the vapor smooth.

As you push higher, into the 500–600°F range, you'll usually get denser clouds and a stronger hit, but the tradeoff is hotter, harsher vapor. Your banger or atomizer will also pick up more dark, burnt residue.

Once you're going past about 600°F, you're basically cooking your material rather than gently vaporizing it — that can taste burnt, feel uncomfortable on your throat and lungs, and leave you with a lot more cleanup.

Why Lower Temperatures Win

Staying on the lower side has big advantages:

  • Better flavor: Terpenes (the compounds that give concentrates their taste and smell) are more noticeable at moderate temps.
  • Smoother hits: Lower temperatures are usually easier on your throat and chest, especially for people who are sensitive to heat.
  • Easier cleaning: When you're not overheating your dabs, your banger, insert, or atomizer is much easier to clean — less black, crusty buildup and more light, wipeable residue.

Temperature Quick Reference

375–450°F — Low Temp (Flavor Zone)

Best flavor and smoothest hits. Ideal for terpene-rich live resins and rosins. Light vapor clouds. Easiest cleanup.

450–500°F — Medium Temp (Balanced)

Good balance of flavor and vapor production. Works well with most concentrate types. Moderate cleanup needed.

500–600°F — High Temp (Cloud Zone)

Dense clouds and stronger effects. Harsher on throat. More residue buildup. Some flavor loss.

600°F+ — Too Hot (Combustion Zone)

Burns material instead of vaporizing. Harsh taste and feel. Heavy black residue. Not recommended.

Finding Your Perfect Temperature

Whether you're using a torch and banger, an e-nail, or a concentrated-herb vaporizer, it's usually smart to start on the lower side (around 375–450°F) and slowly work your way up until you find the temperature range that feels comfortable, tastes good, and gives you the experience you're looking for—without roasting your concentrate or your lungs.

💡 Pro Tip: If you're using a torch, invest in a temperature gun or timer to get consistent results. With e-nails and electric rigs, take advantage of the precise temperature controls — they're there for a reason!

Different Dab Rig Percolation & Recycling

Understanding how water movement affects your dabbing experience.

Dab rigs aren't just "small bongs for concentrates" — the way they move and cool the water (percolation and recycling) has a huge impact on how your hits feel. Percolation breaks your vapor into tiny bubbles to cool and filter it. Recycling moves that water around the piece over and over, keeping it flowing and cool while reducing splashback.

Common Dab Rig Percolator Types

Simple Diffused Downstem / Open Perc

Just a few slits or holes.

Pros: Very easy to clean, low drag, great for flavor chasers.

Cons: Less cooling and filtration than more complex percs.

Showerhead Perc

A vertical tube ending in a flared "showerhead" with slits or holes.

Pros: Smooth, even diffusion; good balance between cooling and flavor.

Cons: Can build up reclaim in the slits if you dab hot or don't clean regularly.

Honeycomb & Disc Percs

Flat discs packed with many small holes.

Pros: Tons of tiny bubbles and nice visual function.

Cons: More surface for reclaim to stick to; can be harder to fully clean.

Matrix / Barrel / Fab-Style Percs

Cylindrical or orb-like percs with multiple slits/holes all around.

Pros: Very smooth hits and strong diffusion, great for people who want soft, cooled vapor.

Cons: More parts = more drag and more cleaning.

What Is a Recycler Dab Rig?

A recycler rig is designed so the water continuously cycles through the piece. You pull in, the water and vapor travel up through one section, then the water "recycles" back down into the main chamber while the vapor goes to your mouthpiece.

Key Benefits of Recycler-Style Rigs

  • Smoother, cooler hits: Constant movement keeps water from going stale and helps cool vapor more evenly.
  • Less splashback: The design channels water away from your mouthpiece, even with strong pulls.
  • Great for flavor: Many recyclers use moderate percs plus gentle recycling, which can preserve terpenes compared to super-aggressive diffusion.

You'll see different recycler styles like external recyclers, incyclers (internal recycling), and multi-arm recyclers. In general, more pathways and percs mean more cooling and smoother hits, but also more drag and more places for reclaim to collect.

Which Should You Choose?

New to Concentrates?

A simple showerhead or small matrix perc rig is usually a great starting point: easy to use, not too draggy, and still smooth.

Ready for the Next Level?

If you love longer, cooler pulls and enjoy watching the function, a recycler dab rig can feel more "advanced" and ritualistic, turning each dab into a little water show while keeping your hits comfortable.
